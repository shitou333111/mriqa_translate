Camera-based Systems for Gating & Motion Correction

How do camera-based methods for motion correction like Philips' VitalEye system work?  
Philip's VitalEye camera to track respiratory motion.

In 2018 Philips' introduced a camera-based system for respiratory gating for MRI named VitalEye. It is still offered as an option on current scanner models. Homegrown camera systems for musculoskeletal MRI and fMRI had been previously reported. That same year, Siemens announced a partnership with KinetiCor to integrate KinetiCor's in-bore camera system and motion correction technology into Siemens' MRI scanners with BioMatrix technology. 
As shown in the photo above, the VitalEye camera is mounted at the rear of the scanner and directed toward the patient's chest where respiratory motion is recorded. Obviously, the system is only effective when the chest is uncovered and not blocked by a surface coil and the line of sight to the chest is not impeded by a head coil or the patient's arms.
Fujifilm has recently begun offering its Synergy Vision system, consisting of two in-bore cameras that capture a continuous video stream throughout the entire scan duration matched to k-space image acquisition. In conjuction with AI-based algorithms, motion-contaminated raw data can be detected and corrected during processing. One clever application in this suite is called StillShot, which allows partial reconstruction of images even when some data in a sequence is completely missing or corrupted (like when a patient climbs out of the scanner!) Some usable image information may still be obtained if the bad raw data is discarded.

Be on the lookout for several optical-based third party products in development. More recently, a Danish company, Tracoinnovations, has received FDA and CE approval of their in-bore 3D-stereo camera (Tracoline) that uses infrared light to capture and correct for not only respiratory motion, but motion of other body parts as well. Currently available for research only, the system can be used in GE, Siemens, and Philips scanners.
