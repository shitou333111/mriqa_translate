Pulse Reversal

Can a −90° pulse reverse the effects of a +90​° pulse?
Theoretically, the full amount of absorbed energy from a +90° RF-pulse can be recovered by application of a −90° RF-pulse. The reason this energy is potentially reversible is because the initial-RF pulse has performed coherent work on the spin system (not heating). System entropy and temperature have remained unchanged. 
For this theoretical result to be obtained, however, the following requirements must be met:
  • Back-to-back pulses with no time lapse between them
  • Pulses applied along the same axis of rotation
  • No energy exchange with the external environment
  • No off-resonance effects
  • No evolution of internal spin couplings​
Of course, in real life none of these ideal conditions exist.  For example, a slight delay (τ) between the pulses will allow free precession of the transverse magnetization to occur, resulting in a phase shift  φ = ωoτ.  Hence a −90° pulse applied along the original axis of rotation will not bring M back to full alignment with Bo unless φ = 0. (Unlike the 180°-refocusing pulses used in spin echo MRI, a −90° pulse can not reverse precession effects nor generate an echo.)

Energy Transfer Back to the RF-Coil

​Although 100% reversible transfer of energy exists only theoretically, in actual practice at least some energy absorbed by the spin system is returned to the RF-coil from which it originated.  ​Here is an outline for the physics of this process: 

Spins produce an oscillating magnetic field in the RF coil.  As per Faraday's Law, this induces a reactive voltage in the coil, sometimes referred to as back-EMF. Energy is thus transferred from spins to the coil and then from the coil to the attached RF electronics where it is absorbed or dissipated as heat. This is a fully coherent, reversible energy exchange, analogous to that observed in a passively driven a driven LC circuit.

​This result should not be viewed as paradoxical. Energy exchange can be bidirectional. The RF-field is not a one-way "energy pump"; it is a dynamic system coupling coils with spins. Note that in the ideal case (very short RF duration, no relaxation effects) energy does not turn directly into heat nor is it radiated away spontaneously.

The "pushing a child on swing" analogy is relevant here. You supply energy to the system when you first push them forward.  As the swing returns to you, some of that initial energy is returned to your muscles as your hands contact the child again before the next push.  After one full cycle, the net energy transfer between you and the child is (theoretically) zero.  This is an example of (partially) reversible energy exchange.
