Remote/Contactless Respiratory Gating

How can respiratory gating be performed without sensors affixed to the patient?  
Over the past decade methods for contactless gating have been developed that do not require dedicated skin electrodes, belts, or attached sensors. At present this system is proprietary to Siemens and can be used for either respiratory gating (called BioMatrix™) or cardiac gating (called Beat Sensor​™). The latter will be discussed in a separate Q&A. 
Contactless gating requires the use of a continuous RF-signal known as the pilot tone (PT) separate from the main RF used for imaging. The signal amplitude of the pilot tone is modulated due to differences in coil loading impedance during heart or chest motion. 

The PT for respiratory gating is transmitted into the patient's chest a dedicated coil located in the spine receiver arrays. Its frequency is offset by about +100 kHz from the system (Larmor) frequency so that it can be easily separated from the main imaging data in the k-space receive coil chain. With calibration and modeling, the PT signal can be converted to a waveform resembling inspiratory phase. 
Pulse-tone in k-space is offset from the main imaging frequency band to allow its extraction.
