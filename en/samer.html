How does the SAMER technique reduce motion artifacts?  
Low-resolution 3D scout from SAMER
Scout Accelerated Motion Estimation and Reduction (SAMER) is a retrospective motion correction protocol offered as a Siemens product.  The technique gets its name from its use of a rapidly acquired (~2 sec) low-resolution 3-plqne scout images obtained once before each pulse sequence as a reference.  Within each TR interval, several (~ 4) additional k-space encoding lines, known as motion guidance lines, are used to estimate 6 degrees of motion (3 rotation, 3 displacement) that may have occurred during that period. After motion parameters across all TR's have been estimated, the motion-corrected images are reconstructed by inverting a generalized forward model that takes into account the previously estimated motion trajectories.  This strategy is very fast since the motion correction optimizations are applied to data confined to each TR interval.  Advanced reconstruction using WAVE-CAIPI and deep learning methods can also be used.
